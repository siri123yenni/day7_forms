7 Wonders of the World

1. Taj Mahal (India)

The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, India.
It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan.
The monument was constructed in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
It is made completely of white marble.
The Taj Mahal is famous for its beautiful architecture.
It changes color depending on sunlight.
It is one of the most visited monuments in the world.

2. Great Wall of China (China)

The Great Wall of China is one of the longest structures ever built.
It was built to protect China from invasions.
The wall stretches over thousands of kilometers.
Construction started over 2000 years ago.
It is made of stone, brick, and earth.
Millions of workers helped build the wall.
It is visible from space according to popular belief.

3. Petra (Jordan)

Petra is an ancient city located in Jordan.
It is carved directly into red sandstone cliffs.
Petra was once a major trading center.
It is also called the "Rose City".
The city is famous for its rock-cut architecture.
Petra was rediscovered in 1812.
It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

4. Christ the Redeemer (Brazil)

Christ the Redeemer is located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
It is a giant statue of Jesus Christ.
The statue stands on Mount Corcovado.
It is 98 feet tall excluding the pedestal.
Construction was completed in 1931.
It is a symbol of peace and Christianity.
The statue attracts millions of visitors each year.

5. Machu Picchu (Peru)

Machu Picchu is located in the Andes Mountains of Peru.
It was built by the Inca civilization.
The city is situated at a high altitude.
It remained hidden for centuries.
Machu Picchu was discovered in 1911.
It is known for its advanced engineering.
It is one of the most mysterious ancient sites.

6. Chichen Itza (Mexico)

Chichen Itza is an ancient Mayan city in Mexico.
It was a major political and religious center.
The main structure is El Castillo pyramid.
The pyramid has 365 steps.
It reflects Mayan astronomical knowledge.
The site shows advanced architecture skills.
It is one of the most visited ruins in Mexico.

7. Colosseum (Italy)

The Colosseum is located in Rome, Italy.
It was built during the Roman Empire.
The Colosseum was used for gladiator fights.
It could hold up to 50,000 spectators.
The structure is made of concrete and stone.
It is over 2000 years old.
It remains a symbol of Roman engineering.
